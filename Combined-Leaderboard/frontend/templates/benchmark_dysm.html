The premise

Correct answers can hide broken perception

29% of a leading model's correct reasoning answers still contained fundamental perception errors. Final-answer accuracy cannot tell you whether a model actually saw the image.

  • Scale1,758 images · 2,612 questions · 7 subtasks
  • GroundingMotor-free human vision tests (TVPS, MVPT)

Findings

Why perception stays broken

23.2 → 41.8

MCQ shortcuts inflate scores

MCQ reformulation nearly doubled GPT-4o's accuracy — models exploit answer options, not the image.

CoT ↓

CoT can hurt

CoT helps verbal tasks (letters, spatial) but hurts holistic ones (figure-ground, closure) where images resist step-by-step description.

~+11%

SFT barely moves the needle

Large-scale fine-tuning gained only ~11% and still fell far short of humans — the limits are foundational.
