The premise

Does text-centric reasoning transfer to space?

Reasoning models excel at math and logic, but spatial reasoning requires geometric intuition and visual grounding — properties that verbose, text-based thinking doesn't provide.

  • Benchmark suiteStatic 2D, 3D geometry, dynamic, and temporal spatial tasks
  • Diagnostic modesstandard, CoT, no-image, and no-image++
  • Main questionWhether reasoning traces improve visual grounding or amplify shortcuts

Evaluation design

One spatial question, four conditions

The paper separates real visual grounding from language priors by rerunning each item under interventions that remove or neutralize the image.

[01]

Standard

Answer from the image and prompt using greedy deterministic decoding.

[02]

CoT

Ask the model to reason step by step and measure whether accuracy improves or drops.

[03]

No-image

Remove the visual input to estimate shortcut learning from textual priors.

[04]

No-image++

Use a blank image plus abstention option to test hallucination resistance.

[05]

Judge

Use GPT-4o to decide whether the final selected option matches ground truth.

Findings

Three robustness diagnostics

Beyond raw accuracy, this track measures how a model arrives at its answer — surfacing reasoning that games the test.

CoT-Δ

CoT degrades spatial accuracy

CoT reduces accuracy by up to −23% for some reasoning models. Higher (less negative) is better.

No-image

Shortcut learning

Accuracy without the image measures how much textual bias drives answers instead of visual grounding.

No-Image++

Hallucination resistance

With a blank image and a "Cannot determine" option, reasoning models often hallucinate spatial details instead of abstaining.
