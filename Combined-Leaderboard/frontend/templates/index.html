Robustness diagnostics

  • CoT Δ — accuracy change when forced to reason step-by-step. Negative = CoT hurts spatial reasoning (the paper's core finding).
  • Shortcut score — accuracy on a gray image. High = guessing from text priors. Lower is better.
  • Hallucination resistance — on gray-image items where “Cannot determine” is correct, how often the model abstains. Higher is better.

Diagnostics populate when a Task-3 submission includes the optional cot, no_image and no_image_plus conditions produced by the harness.

About

This leaderboard evaluates visual intelligence on two independent tracks built from three separately-submitted tasks.

Track 1 — Visual Cognition

  • Do-You-See-Me (Perception) — low-level visual discrimination: spatial relations, form constancy, figure-ground, visual closure, letter and shape disambiguation.
  • Mind's-Eye (Imagery) — internal manipulation of visual representations: mental rotation, paper folding, mental composition, isomorphism reasoning.

The two task accuracies combine (equal weight) into the Visual Cognition Index (VCI). Each is a pure accuracy score; diagnostics live entirely in Track 2.

Track 2 — Spatial Reasoning Analysis

A combined evaluation over 13 public spatial benchmarks (arXiv:2604.16060). Reported as accuracy plus robustness diagnostics: CoT Δ, shortcut (gray-image), and hallucination resistance (gray-image + abstention option). Because some sources (e.g. RealWorldQA, CC BY-ND) forbid redistribution, we publish a manifest + harness rather than the data: you run the harness locally and upload one response file.
