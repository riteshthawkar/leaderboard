The premise

Can models build and manipulate mental images?

Can a model generate visual states not in the input — rotating a 3D object, predicting a fold, composing shapes? This is fluid spatial intelligence, and it remains an open question for MLLMs.

  • Task familyVisual abstraction, transformation, and composition
  • Question styleMultiple choice with 4-6 candidate answers
  • Core diagnosisModels localize image evidence but fail to mentally simulate it

The taxonomy

Abstraction · Relation · Transformation

Eight tasks span three complementary facets of visuo-cognitive reasoning.

[A]
Abstract

Inferring latent structure

Pattern induction across configurations — in the spirit of Raven's Progressive Matrices. Covers abstract pattern completion and hierarchical isomorphs.

[R]
Relate

Mapping correspondences

Analogical mapping across configurations — dynamic and symmetric isomorphs, plus slippage (violation detection).

[T]
Transform

Manipulating structure

Mental rotation, paper folding, and shape composition — the generative operations central to spatial intelligence.

Findings

Where visuo-cognition breaks down

Transform ↓

Simulation is missing

Failures cluster on Transformation and Abstraction — consistent with absent mental simulation, not just difficulty.

Prompt ±

Scaffolding cuts both ways

Structured prompting helps Abstraction but consistently hurts Transformation — rule derivation vs. mental simulation.

Look ≠ reason

They look but can't reason

Models localize the right image regions but fail to reason over them — they find where to look, not how to reason.
